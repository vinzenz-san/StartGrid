Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-22 (Gmail integration removed)

StartGrid is a browser extension (Firefox & Chrome) that replaces your new tab page with a customizable widget grid. This policy explains what data StartGrid accesses, why, and how it's stored — in particular for the optional Google Calendar widget, which requires signing in with your Google Account.

Summary

  • StartGrid does not operate a backend server that stores your personal data. All settings, cached widget data, and authentication tokens are stored locally in your browser's extension storage.
  • Google Account access is entirely optional, off by default, and only activates when you click "Connect" inside the Calendar widget's settings.
  • All Google data access is read-only. StartGrid never sends, deletes, modifies, or shares your calendar data.
  • StartGrid does not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties or advertisers.

Data accessed via Google Sign-In

When you click "Connect" in the Calendar widget, StartGrid opens Google's standard OAuth consent screen. If you approve, StartGrid receives an access token scoped to only the permissions listed below — nothing else in your Google Account is touched.

ScopeWhat it's used for
calendar.readonly Fetches upcoming events from your primary calendar — title, start/end time, location, color, and a link back to the event — via a field-restricted Calendar API request. Used to render the Calendar widget. No event is ever created, edited, or deleted.
openid, email Used only to show which Google Account is currently connected in the widget's settings panel (e.g. "Connected as name@example.com"), so you can confirm or switch accounts.

Where this data is stored

Your Google access token, refresh token (if issued), and the fetched event previews are cached only in your browser's local extension storage (browser.storage.local), on your own device. They are never transmitted to any server operated by StartGrid or its developer. Disconnecting the account (or uninstalling the extension) removes this data immediately.

Bookmarks and tabs

The Bookmark Folder, Bookmark Search, and Quicklinks widgets read your browser's bookmark tree (bookmarks permission) to display and let you navigate your bookmarks from the new tab page. This data is read locally and never transmitted anywhere. Clicking a bookmark or quicklink opens it using the tabs permission (tabs.create / tabs.update) — StartGrid does not otherwise read your open tabs' URLs, titles, or content.

Location (Weather widget)

The optional Weather widget, if you enable it and grant permission, uses your browser's geolocation API to get your device's coordinates. These coordinates are sent directly from your browser to the third-party weather provider Open-Meteo to fetch local weather data — StartGrid's developer never receives or stores this location data. You can alternatively search for a location by name instead of sharing your device's position, and you can deny or revoke the location permission at any time via your browser's site permissions for this extension.

Other data StartGrid accesses

Optional background-image widgets (Unsplash, NASA Astronomy Picture of the Day, Bing, Wikimedia) fetch a public photo URL and its attribution text from each provider's API — no personal or Google Account data is involved in these requests. Some requests are routed through a Cloudflare Worker that StartGrid's developer operates solely to keep third-party API keys out of the extension's source code; this Worker does not log, store, or inspect request contents beyond what's needed to forward the call.

Your controls

  • Each widget's Google connection can be disconnected at any time from its settings panel, which revokes the token with Google and clears all locally stored data for that widget.
  • You can also revoke StartGrid's access at any time from your Google Account permissions page.
  • Uninstalling the extension removes all locally stored data.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "Last updated" date above will be revised and, for material changes, noted in the extension's release notes.
